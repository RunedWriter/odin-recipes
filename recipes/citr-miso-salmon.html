Citrus-Miso Salmon

(Please click on the image above to follow the link to the original site and recipe!)

Flacky, delicious salmon served with citrus and miso. Bang bang-roasted brussles served on side.

"Sometimes, there's such a good idea right under our noses, that we're surprised we hadn't thought of it before. Like pairing a flaky, perfect salmon withâ€¦ citrus and miso? Lemon and lime, two stalwart citrus stars, combine with earthy miso paste to form a dressing-like sauce that's like manna from heaven. The other thought that came to us was combining bang bang sauce, known for its pairing with shrimp, with Brussels sprouts, giving the veggies a bit of a kick." - Chef Nigel Palmer. Prep and cook time comes to 30-40 min, recipe serves two, spice level mild, difficulty intermediate.

Ingredients

  • 12 oz Brussels sprouts
  • 12 oz salmon fillets
  • 1 lemon
  • 1 lime
  • 1 tbsp white miso paste
  • 1/2 oz honey
  • .42 oz mayonnaise
  • 2 tsp sriracha
  • 1/4 oz cilantro

Steps

Before you cook

You will need olive oil, salt, pepper, cooking spray, a baking sheet, 2 mixing bowls and 1 medium non-stick pan.

  1. Pre-heat oven to 450 F.
  2. Remove all produce from any packaging or contraints, then thoroughly rinse and pat dry.
  3. Prepare a baking sheet with foil and cooking spray.

Prepare the ingredients

  1. Trim stems off Brussels sprouts and halve vertically (quarter if larger than a ping-pong ball).
  2. Zest lemon, halve, and juice. Zest only the outside part; avoid white pith underneath skin.
  3. Zest lime, halve, and juice.
  4. Mince cilantro (no need to stem).
  5. Pat salmon fillets dry, and seaon flesh side with 1/4 tsp. salt and a pinch of pepper.

Roast the Brussels sprouts

  1. Place Brussels sprouts on prepared baking sheet and toss with 1 Tbsp. olive oil, 1/4 tsp. salt, and a pinch of pepper.
  2. Massage oil into sprouts.
  3. Spread into a single layer, cut-side down, and roast in hot oven until tender and browned, 14-16 minutes.
  4. While Brussels sprouts roast, make bang bang sauce.

Make the bang bang sauce

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine mayonnaise and Sriracha (to taste). Set aside.

Make the citrus-miso sauce

  1. In another mixing bowl, combine miso, honey, 1/4 tsp. lemon zest, and 1/4 tsp. lime zest until miso is dissolved and sauce is smooth.
  2. Stir in 11/2 tsp. lemon juice, 11/2 tsp. lime juice, 1 tsp. olive oil, and a pinch of salt and pepper. Set aside.

Cook salmon and finish dish

  1. Place a medium non-stick pan over medium heat and add 1 tsp. olive oil. Add salmon, skin side up, to hot pan and cook until salmon reaches a minimum internal temperature of 145 degrees, 4-6 minutes per side.
  2. Remove from burner
  3. Plate dish as pictured on front of card, placing salmon on citrus-miso sauce and garnishing Brussels sprouts with bang bang sauce and cilantro.
  4. Enjoy!
